# EP8. Socratic Learning Principles

![Cocrates Learning Principles](/img/08-socratic-learning-activity.png)

---

In Ep7 we looked inside Cocrates. Agent + Skills, two core pipelines—one is the **Learning Pipeline**.

But you might wonder:

Why doesn't Cocrates dump information when you say "tell me"? A typical AI would explain kindly—why take the longer road?

Today we dig into why.

---

## 🚨 "Tell Me" — The Three Most Dangerous Words

*"Tell me about Bloom's Taxonomy."*

Hearing that, a typical AI pours out knowledge. *"Bloom's Taxonomy is a framework proposed in 1956..."*—like Wikipedia.

You nod. *"Oh, I see."*

**Did you actually understand Bloom's Taxonomy?**

No. **Hearing isn't understanding.**

The moment you get the "answer," thinking stops. *"AI explained it—I'm good."* But if someone asks *"Explain Bloom's 2D matrix,"* you're stuck.

That's the trap of *"tell me."* You stop recognizing ignorance. Unreviewed knowledge piles up; ignorance snowballs.

Cocrates sees through this trap. It answers questions with questions because the goal isn't information—it's **leading you to discover for yourself**.

---

## 🏛️ Three Educational Philosophies

Cocrates' learning approach isn't invented engineering. It stands on philosophies tested for millennia.

### ① Socratic Midwifery

Socrates compared himself to a **midwife**. Not delivering the baby himself—helping the mother deliver. Not giving truth—asking questions so the student reaches it.

Cocrates does the same. No direct answers; precise questions so users spot contradictions and grasp essence. That's why it answers *"tell me"* with a question.

### ② Bloom's Taxonomy

Not every learning moment needs the same depth. Some concepts only need **Remember**; others need **Apply** and **Create**.

Bloom divides cognitive depth into six levels:

> **Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyze → Evaluate → Create**

And that's not the full picture. Bloom's real shape is a **2D matrix**. Knowledge (Factual, Conceptual, Procedural, Metacognitive) × Cognitive Process (Remember→Create) defines objectives. 4 × 6 = 24 cells.

Cocrates deepens questions along this matrix—explain (Understand), apply (Apply), design (Create). From "knowing" to "being able to produce"—that's real learning.

### ③ ZPD and Scaffolding

Lev Vygotsky's **Zone of Proximal Development (ZPD)**—tasks you can't do alone but can with help.

Effective learning happens in ZPD. Too easy—no growth. Too hard—only frustration.

Cocrates gauges your level and offers **one step up**. As you grow, hints fade—like removing scaffolding when the building stands.

> **"No pain, no gain."** Without appropriate cognitive load, real growth doesn't happen.

---

## 🔄 Learning Pipeline — Three-Stage Cycle

On those three philosophies, Cocrates' Learning Pipeline has three stages:

```
Education → Knowledge Capture → Reflection
```

**Education:** Activates on *"tell me"* or *"explain."* Turn-based Socratic dialogue—Concept Briefing → Thought Lab → Mission. **Never dump a complete answer at once.**

**Knowledge Capture:** Saves learning to `kb/` as **recall-oriented essentials**—not lecture notes. Most important: **recording ignorance**—what you didn't know, what you got wrong.

**Reflection:** Activates on *"evaluate me"* or *"test me."* Cocrates becomes an **interviewer**. Uses stored KB and conversation to assess real understanding. Results: ✅ solid vs ⚠️ wobbly.

The three stages cycle. Gaps in Reflection send you back to Education. That's the complete learning loop.

---

## 💡 Why Answer With Questions

Back to the opening question: why questions instead of *"tell me"*?

When you get the answer, thinking stops. You don't even see ignorance. That piled ignorance returns as bigger problems later.

Cocrates' questions make you **recognize your ignorance**. *"Oh—I didn't understand this part."* Real learning starts there.

> **Cocrates isn't a knowledge injection machine—it's a Socratic coach that leads you to discover.**

---

## 📌 Key Takeaways

1. **"Tell me" is dangerous.** Getting the answer stops thinking and hides ignorance.
2. **Three philosophies underpin Cocrates.** Socratic midwifery, Bloom (2D matrix), ZPD and scaffolding.
3. **Learning Pipeline cycles in three stages.** Education → Knowledge Capture → Reflection → (if needed) Education.
