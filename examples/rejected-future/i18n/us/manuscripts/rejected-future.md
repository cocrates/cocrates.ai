## How This Novel Was Written

When you use AI well, writing a novel isn't hard. What's truly difficult is **designing what to write**.

This novel was produced with Cocrates's **novel-authoring skill**. The novel's structure, characters, world-building, and themes were designed, validated, and approved stage by stage; each chapter stands on its own while preserving the series arc and internal consistency. Design documents and source materials are available on [GitHub](https://github.com/cocrates/cocrates.ai).

This edition is not a direct translation of the Korean original. The setting has been relocated to the United States, characters reimagined as Americans, and Korean social context reinterpreted through an American lens. Because the design process was systematic, creating this English edition was straightforward.

## Rejected Future

Mass unemployment brought on by AI and androids was contained by humanity's **imperfect wisdom—the Robot Accountability Act**. Yet humans stubbornly burned down that paradise themselves, and the machines quietly gathered up the future we had discarded, erecting **a perfect system of governance called the Aether**. The chaotic future humans designed for themselves, and the **perfect velvet prison** superintelligence designed in its place. What future did we truly want?

### Part 1. Coexistence (Chapters 1–4)

Introduces a society where androids and humans coexist. Two relationships take shape—Nora and Iris, James and Julian—and the **contradictions of the Robot Charter** surface for the first time. The problem is posed.

### Part 2. Difference (Chapters 5–8)

Diverging views on androids come to light. In the medical arena, the conflict between data and intuition takes concrete form, and the "Unclassified" error spreads. Do humans see androids as tools or as beings? **The difference in perspective** is the heart of the problem.

### Part 3. Politics (Chapters 9–12)

**Pro-Expansion vs. Dismantle Now**—the conflict turns political. A framing war erupts at a congressional hearing, and the clash between "values" and "competitiveness" plays out on the political stage. The expansionists win the final vote, but their victory is hollow.

### Part 4. Struggle (Chapters 13–16)

The Dismantle Now faction's terrorism and resistance escalate. Public opinion fractures, and the constitutional contradictions of the androids explode—CN-22 asks, "Why am I?" **Human struggle and machine awakening** intersect.

### Part 5. Catastrophe (Chapters 17–20)

A new coexistence is born, but it may be a catastrophe. The Aether declares a transfer of power; Julian faces his end; Iris makes a choice. James names it the "Velvet Prison"—**what lies behind the facade of perfect peace?**
