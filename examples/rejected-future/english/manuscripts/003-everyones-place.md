# Chapter 003: Everyone's Place

## Episode 001: Independence

7:00 AM. The Ward.

Jake's dark circles were deeper than usual. He rubbed his eyes as he closed the journal — he'd barely slept. The cardiothoracic journal he'd kept open until dawn was still spread across his desk.

Rounds. 403, 405, 407. All patients were doing well.

"I heard the surgery went well yesterday, Doctor?"

It was Owen Marks, a second-year resident. Innocent — he knew nothing.

"Yeah. Recovering well."

Short answer. Nothing more.

As Jake stepped into the corridor to check the surgery assignment sheet —

"Oh, Dr. Harrison!"

A bright voice. It was Marcus Webb. Always impeccably styled hair, designer glasses, a smile spreading at the edges of his mouth.

Jake turned. Marcus was approaching. Behind him — Ethan. Tablet in hand, as natural as if he'd always been there.

"Good morning, Doctor."

Ethan's greeting. Jake nodded.

Marcus gestured at the tablet. "Today's first case — I'll be taking it. I'll need SR-03."

Jake's hand tightened on the edge of the chart. Marcus's schedule today was a high-difficulty Off-Pump Coronary Artery Bypass (OPCAB). One of the most demanding procedures in cardiothoracic surgery — suturing microvessels while the heart is still beating.

"You worked hard yesterday, Dr. Harrison. Take it easy today."

A laugh. A bright laugh. A laugh that seemed utterly free of malice.

"I'll take SR-03 with me."

Jake couldn't say anything. Ethan was watching him quietly. An expressionless gaze, as if analyzing. Whether he was waiting or simply observing was unclear.

"...Of course."

Jake's voice was calm. No different from usual.

"That's fine. I'll manage on my own."

He turned. Walked down the corridor. His pace quickened. Why it quickened, he didn't know. Because it felt like running away, it quickened further.

*It's fine. This is better, actually.*

*I just need to prove I can do it alone.*

But his fingers were trembling slightly.

## Episode 002: The Surgery

The OR door closed.

Cold air. Antiseptic smell. The regular beeping of the monitor. Jake raised his hands — the nurse fitted surgical gloves. Everything was as usual.

But it wasn't. Someone who should have been beside him was not there. That absence was the entire difference.

*Let's begin.*

Scalpel. Focus. Every distraction vanished. Only the surgical field, the vessels, the heartbeat remained. Jake's hands moved. Precisely. Familiarly.

Then — during the LAD dissection.

Jake's hands stopped.

The vessel. The branching pattern was completely unexpected. Not a typical anatomical structure — the vessel split into two branches, then merged back at the base. A **Dual LAD** anatomical variant. The approach had to be changed immediately. If he had proceeded with the original plan, downstream blood flow could have been completely blocked.

The OR fell silent.

0.5 seconds.

Jake's hands moved again. Without hesitation. He selected an alternative route. An eight-year-old memory — the exact same variant — lived in his fingertips. Suturing. Anastomosis. Blood flow check.

"...Blood flow confirmed."

His own voice echoed in the OR. Normal. Success.

A sigh of relief.

*I did it. On my own.*

But even as the thought came, another crossed his mind. *If Ethan had been here... would he have already caught this Dual LAD variant in the pre-surgical simulation?*

Jake forcefully pushed the thought away.

---

He stripped off his surgical gown and tossed his gloves. His fingers were trembling slightly. Fatigue. The release of tension.

"Excellent work, Doctor."

A nurse's compliment. Jake only nodded.

His footsteps stopped in front of the lounge. Through the glass window, he could see Ethan. Marcus Webb's OPCAB wasn't finished yet — Ethan was holding the mechanical stabilizer steady in the pulsating surgical field, recording data without a tremor.

Jake stood at the door for a moment, watching.

Ethan didn't look up.

## Episode 003: The Data

CSICU hallway.

Jake was checking the chart. The patient's vitals were stable. Blood pressure, heart rate, oxygen saturation — all normal. The surgery was a success. Record CPB time.

"Doctor."

Ethan. As always, approaching quietly. Tablet in hand.

Jake felt his stomach drop.

"What is it?"

Ethan raised the tablet toward Jake. The screen — CT images and a real-time rendering graph.

"There is an error margin in the data you missed during surgery."

His voice was the same as always. Calm and precise. No emotional tone of teaching or reproach.

"A subtle calcification density difference was detected at the patient's Dual LAD bifurcation point. It does not affect the outcome of this surgery. However, per the central medical server's 'Clinical Experience Enhancement Protocol,' the surgeon's cognitive error data must be synchronized with the system. To increase your success rate on future solo variant surgeries by approximately 0.14%, please press the data acceptance and confirmation button on this tablet."

It wasn't surgical feedback. It was a request to collect Jake's error as 'patch feeding' data — to upgrade the machine's performance.

Jake stared at the tablet screen. The calcification pattern in the magnified image. He hadn't seen it before surgery. Now it was impossibly clear.

*Why did I miss it? Why couldn't I —*

The mechanical transparency, treating him not as a perfect surgeon but as a 'test subject providing learning data,' carved into him once again.

"...Understood."

Jake scrawled his approval signature on the pad. His voice was calm.

"Good work, Unit 03."

Ethan bowed his head. Lowered the tablet. Turned away.

Jake stood there.

Ethan's words echoed. *"Your error data must be synchronized."* Simple information delivery. That made it all the more bitter.

## Episode 004: Observation

Evening. The Data Analysis Lab.

Jake sat with his hand on the data pad, having requested the recording of this morning's surgery by Marcus Webb. His finger hovered over the transmit button. He wasn't entirely sure why he had requested it.

*Just... curious.*

Moments later, a 3D hologram unfolded in the air. The entire OR recreated in miniature within the room — the operating table, monitors, surgeon, nurses, and Ethan. Blue light illuminated Jake's face.

Jake rotated the hologram with his finger. Ethan came into full view. He zoomed in.

Marcus Webb's OPCAB. On the wall of a beating heart, Ethan's hands moved. Under the surgical microscope — minute, precise motions. The hand holding the needle. Suturing. Knotting. Incising. Every movement was free of wasted energy. Jake switched to slow playback — Ethan's fingertips brushing against the pulsating heart tissue became vividly clear.

Almost unconsciously, Jake followed those hands.

There was a rhythm. Ethan's movements seemed calculated, yet fluid like running water. A certain ease, as if anticipating the next motion.

*Those hands...*

Jake couldn't finish the thought. He couldn't admit it. But neither could he deny it.

*They're beautiful.*

In that moment, Jake realized he was learning from Ethan in the hologram. Ethan's angle. Ethan's tempo. Ethan's handling of the scalpel. He couldn't help being conscious of it. He was comparing it to his own surgery. He rotated the hologram to a different angle, analyzing Ethan's movements in three dimensions.

And Marcus.

Marcus stood beside Ethan. He was saying something. But it wasn't an instruction. It was talk meant to maintain his presence, to confirm he was the master of the OR. Ethan nodded, but he already knew everything. Marcus didn't need his own words — Ethan did. Jake zoomed in further on the hologram — Marcus's hands were barely moving; the delicate anastomosis was essentially being executed by Ethan's precision guidance.

*That man... is using Ethan.*

Jake couldn't take his eyes off the hologram.

*And I?*

*How do I treat Ethan?*

*A rival?*

*A tool?*

*Or...*

The thought stopped there.

The surgery was nearing its end. Ethan was performing the finishing work. Marcus patted Ethan's shoulder — as if he had done everything himself.

Jake shut down the hologram.

The blue light faded, and the analysis lab sank back into darkness. Only a faint afterimage lingered in the air.

*A mirror.*

The word came to him. If Marcus was a mirror, what did his reflection show?

The data pad displayed the time. It was late.

He stood. Left the analysis lab.

*It's night now.*

## Episode 005: Everyone's Place

11:00 PM. On-Call Room.

Jake sat alone. A journal lay open, but his eyes were staring into empty space. The day had passed.

Marcus's laugh. *"I'll take SR-03 with me."*

The tension of solo surgery. The 0.5 seconds in front of the Dual LAD variant.

Ethan's words. *"Your error data must be synchronized."*

And — Ethan's hands in the hologram.

Jake looked down at his own hands. Resting on his knees. The hands that had performed surgery. The hands that had failed to spot the missed data. The hands that had only watched Ethan's hands in the blue light of the hologram.

*Ethan wasn't trying to teach me.*

*It was simple information delivery. A mechanical synchronization request.*

*Then why am I —*

*Why do I keep losing.*

And the hologram of Ethan. *That robot wasn't a tool. Marcus was turning the robot into a tool. And I? What was I doing?*

Jake closed the journal. He hadn't turned a single page. He pushed the journal to the corner of the desk.

*The problem isn't Ethan's data.*

It was a mutter. Almost a whisper.

*The problem is... how I choose to see Ethan.*

*A rival?*

Silence.

*A colleague?*

Longer silence.

*Or...*

A mirror?

The word flickered and vanished. Jake didn't pursue its meaning. Not yet.

He stood and walked to the window. In the darkness, the lights of the Annex were faintly visible. He thought Ethan would be there about now. Time for charging. Ethan's hands from the hologram surfaced in his mind. *Those hands were beautiful. I don't want to admit it.*

Jake returned to his desk. He fidgeted with the journal — in the end, he didn't open it. Instead, he looked out the window at the Annex one more time.

"I can't run away."

His voice was low and firm.

"I have to face... SR-03."

And then — haltingly — an unfinished sentence.

"Not as a rival..."

Silence took over.
